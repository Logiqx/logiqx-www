  <h1>25 January 2003</h1>

  <table class="news">
    <tr>
      <td>
        <p>I have finally found time to update to my <a href=
        "HowTo/">compile guides</a> for MAME and
        RAINE!</p>

        <p>These guides have been written for the level of
        understanding that I like to have myself.<br />
        If you want to understand a little more about what is being
        used by MAME and RAINE then you may find them useful.<br />
        If you want don't care what everything is and don't want to
        compile RAINE then just use the downloads at the MAME page.
        ;)</p>

        <p>The biggest changes in this update are:</p>

        <p>1. Both the DJGPP and <a href=
        "HowTo/">MinGW</a> environments use GCC 3
        (previously they used GCC 2).<br />
        2. You can create a single <a href=
        "HowTo/">MinGW</a> environment that works with
        MAME and RAINE32 (no more DirectX conflicts).<br />
        3. The DJGPP environment now works under NT and XP (no more
        'Out of DOS memory', 'No DPMI selectors', etc).</p>
      </td>
    </tr>
  </table>
