Emulators like MAME often use the CRC32 algorithm to identify game ROMs and I sometimes wonder whether there are any conflicts yet (i.e. where two differing ROMs have the same CRC). MAMEMD5 has been written to find this out!

Although CRC32 is extremely good for checking if a file has been corrupted during transmission (there is only a 1 in 4 billion chance of a corruption not affecting the CRC) the chance of two files in a large group having CRCs that match is actually higher than expected.

For MAME v0.54 I calculate a probability of 34.119769% (when considering complemented CRC functionality which effectively doubles the number of CRCs used). However, this is ignoring the ROM sizes which being known reduce these odds to 4.000046%. Rob has kindly drawn up some pretty charts to show the probability in relation to the number of ROMs; you can choose between a close up or overall view.

There are just two ROMs in MAME that currently share the same CRC (albeit through complementing):

chukatai: 128KB ROM with CRC of 6cb1e8fc
ninjemak/youma: 32KB ROM with CRC of 934e1703 (which has a complement of 6cb1e8fc)

What I am more interested to know is if any games have ROMs with the same size and matching CRCs but differing contents. It is also possible that non-genuine files have accidentally got into the scene and this tool should spot them. If you run MAMEMD5 over your sets and it reports some kind of discrepancy then you have found what I wish to know!

Please take part in my little experiment and let me know your findings!

Results so far: 16 without conflicts (v1.2), 4 without conflicts (not sure of version).
