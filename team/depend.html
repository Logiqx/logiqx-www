How to read this document!

Point 1: Firstly, all possible dependencies are listed under the relevant section header. For example, when you need to know which dats are dependant on a MAME release you only need to read the MAME section, NOT the CPS-1, CPS-2 or Neo-Geo sections. For example, whilst the CPS-2 supplement is dependant on the MAME dat, the CPS-2 dat itself is not so DO NOT read the CPS-2 section. This is significant because jumping from the MAME section to the CPS-2 section would make you check Calice which is completely unrelated to MAME. Everything that can possibly be affected by MAME is listed under the MAME section. There are rare occasions when the CPS-2 dat and MAME are updated at the same time but do not mistake that for MAME forcing updates to the CPS-2 dat, it is not. Under such circumstances, both the MAME and CPS-2 sections are relevant since both dats have been updated independantly.

Point 2: When reading the dependencies (which are hierarchical), as soon as you discover that a dat has not changed then stop reading down that branch of the hierarchy. For example, if you are processing a MAME update and you discover that the CPS-1 dat (semi-automatic) has not changed then do not check it's dependants (sound dats and CPS-1 supplement) since they can not have changed. However, the sound emulator dats may still change as a consequence of Neo-Geo related changes (as can be seen from the listed dependencies).
